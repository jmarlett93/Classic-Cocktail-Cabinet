# Impeccable — Classic Cocktail Cabinet

## Design Context

### Users

Primary audience is **home bartenders and enthusiasts**: curious, flavor-driven, and often deciding under mild time pressure (shopping, party prep, or relaxed exploration at home). They move between **phone** (store aisle, kitchen pocket), **laptop** (research and planning), and **social/party** contexts where answers need to feel fast and trustworthy, not homework.

The core job is **effortless guidance** in two modes:

1. **Purchase-oriented liquor recommendations** shaped by stated preferences, budget, and taste goals.
2. **Recipe discovery** that respects how they like drinks to taste—not generic lists, but answers that feel tailored and decisive.

### Brand Personality

**Voice:** warm expert, never condescending; confident shorthand is fine when it saves time.

**Three-word personality (actionable):** **assured**, **sensorial**, **hospitable**.

(Your words *elegant*, *cool*, and *sophisticated* map here as *refined surfaces*, *quiet confidence*, and *adult leisure*—not stiff formality, not nightlife cliché.)

**Emotional goals:** the user should feel *relieved* (“that was easy”), *seen* (“it got my taste”), and *slightly elevated* (“I’m treating myself well”) without irony or gimmick.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** **playful luxury**—generous whitespace and **airy** layouts, **script typography** used as accent and occasion (labels, hero moments, small celebrations of a match), and a **multichromatic** palette where color **encodes taste and category** (citrus, herbal, spirit families) rather than decorating at random. Motion and detail should feel **intentional and light**, not noisy.

**Theme:** **Cool by default, not “dark mode by default.”** Prefer surfaces and neutrals that read as crisp and contemporary (slightly cool-tinted neutrals, clear hierarchy) with optional deeper theme later if it serves low-light use—avoid equating “sophisticated” with dim UI alone.

**References (interpretation, not copies):** high-end bar menus and **editorial drink photography**—breathing room, hierarchy, and flavor as story; **tasting notes** as a metaphor for UI copy and color accents.

**Anti-references:** generic chatbot chrome, stock “neon cocktail” clichés, dense dashboard grids, and **palette lock-in** to the legacy pink-forward scheme unless it still serves the story—you are **not married to the current palette**; evolve it toward taste-led color.

### Design Principles

1. **One clear outcome per beat** — recommendation or recipe path surfaces the answer first; supporting detail unfolds without burying the decision.
2. **Taste is visible** — use color, micro-labels, and light illustration to suggest flavor families; keep multichrome **meaningful**, not decorative noise.
3. **Luxury through restraint** — fewer, stronger type sizes; ample rhythm; script only where it signals *occasion* or *identity*, not everywhere.
4. **Cool clarity** — default UI reads bright and composed; contrast and focus states stay sharp on phone and laptop.
5. **Inclusive calm** — respect **prefers-reduced-motion**, keep text/background contrast strong as flavors get more expressive, and do not rely on color alone for critical distinctions.
